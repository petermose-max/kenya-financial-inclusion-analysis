# Report Summary: Kenya Financial Inclusion Analysis 2024

The SQL phase of this project shows that Kenya has achieved broad financial inclusion through mobile money, but important gaps remain in formal banking, insurance, financial health, and resilience. National mobile money use is 78.69%, bank use is 45.52%, insurance use is 19.25%, and overall access is 81.43% under the working SQL definition.

The strongest county-level performance appears in Nairobi, Kiambu, Embu, Nyeri, and Elgeyo-Marakwet, while the highest exclusion risk appears in West Pokot, Turkana, Tana River, Narok, and Migori. Income is a major dividing line: overall access rises from 68.57% in the lowest income quintile to 92.83% in the highest income quintile, while bank access rises from 19.24% to 75.80%.

Policy priorities should include county-specific targeting, deeper focus on financial resilience rather than access alone, and segmentation that combines geography, gender, and income. Fintechs should design low-cost mobile-first products for irregular-income users, use county-level segmentation for rollout strategy, and treat gender and financial literacy gaps as product design constraints.

The next phase is Python exploratory data analysis, including visualisation, mapping, survey-weighted indicators, demographic comparisons, and dashboard-ready outputs.
